Digital Feature: The AI Shift

1. UNESCO’s Vision: AI for the Global Good

Artificial Intelligence (AI) holds significant promise for transforming education today by addressing key challenges and furthering the Sustainable Development Goal 4 (SDG 4).


The swift advancement of technology presents numerous risks that have outpaced existing policy discussions. UNESCO is currently at the forefront of facilitating Member States in leveraging AI technologies to realize the Education 2030 Agenda. They emphasize that the integration of AI in education must adhere to fundamental principles of inclusion and equity.


UNESCO advocates for a human-centered approach to AI. The goal is to redirect discussions to how these tools can mitigate inequalities in access to information and research. This ensures that the representation of diverse cultural expressions is not erased by standardized algorithms.


The vision of "AI for all" is designed so that everyone benefits from the ongoing technological revolution. By accessing innovation and knowledge through frameworks like the "Beijing Consensus," policy-makers are better equipped to navigate this complex landscape without leaving vulnerable populations behind.


Furthermore, UNESCO has introduced specific competency frameworks tailored for both students and educators. These are designed to help countries recognize both the potential benefits and the inherent risks associated with AI, fostering a collective understanding of digital safety.

Conclusion: Human rights and dignity must remain at the center of every digital education policy to ensure a fair future for all learners.

2. The Preparedness Gap in PH Schools

Filipino educational institutions are progressively adopting AI-driven technologies to enhance administrative efficiency and personalize learning experiences for the youth.


According to Ryan Lufkin of Instructure, tools like Eduaide.ai are already helping educators design instructional materials, while platforms like NotebookLM convert content into podcasts. However, despite this growing enthusiasm, the confidence levels among the academic community remain surprisingly lukewarm.


The "State of Higher Education 2025" report reveals a stark reality: only 48% of educators feel adequately prepared to train students for future workforce demands. Similarly, only 50% of students believe their institutions can truly equip them for an AI-centric job market.


Workplaces are evolving so rapidly that organizations are now investing in reskilling their employees every 3 to 5 years. This shift highlights the necessity for lifelong learning, where traditional education paradigms are giving way to continuous, modular skill enhancement.


Students are urged to engage with free AI courses from top universities. Early exposure to these tools positions them as pivotal contributors in shaping future workplace dynamics, rather than just passive consumers of technology.


Conclusion: Bridging the gap between technological adoption and user confidence is the next big challenge for Philippine schools.

3. E-CAIR: The Heart of AI Research

On February 20, 2025, the Department of Education officially inaugurated the Education Center for AI Research (E-CAIR) in Pasig City to foster innovation.


Building upon foundational work initiated in 2024, E-CAIR is the first dedicated hub for AI research in the country. Its core focus is to enhance basic education by developing tools that improve teaching, learning, and school management, aligning with President Ferdinand R. Marcos Jr.’s modernization vision.


The center utilizes data-driven mapping through the Adopt-A-School Program to pinpoint schools requiring infrastructure enhancement. It also aims to facilitate the generation of computer-based examination results and provide insights for the National Qualifying Examination for School Heads (NQESH).


Current projects include using computer vision to identify stunted learners and those with disabilities. E-CAIR is also working on natural hazards mapping to ensure that educational institutions are better prepared for environmental challenges and disasters.


Education Secretary Sonny Angara remarked that this initiative is not merely about being tech-savvy. It is a commitment to using AI for genuine and lasting reforms that reflect the ethos of "Bagong Pilipinas," making the Philippines a hub for AI in Southeast Asia.


Conclusion: E-CAIR is setting the stage for a data-driven transformation that prioritizes student welfare and administrative precision.

4. DepEd Order No. 003: The Rules of AI

The Department of Education has officially permitted the use of AI in public schools following the release of Department Order No. 003, series of 2026.


These guidelines permit personnel and students to explore new tools like ChatGPT, Google Gemini, and Canva, provided they align with ethical and human-centered standards. The goal is to support teaching without replacing the central role of human judgment in evaluations.


Applications are categorized by risk level. While grammar checkers are "minimal risk," applications for grading, admissions, and scholarships are "high-risk." These high-risk tools require strict human oversight to prevent bias and ensure fairness for all students.


Students must now disclose their use of AI in their work. This includes providing citations and descriptions of how tools were used for brainstorming or writing support. This transparency is vital for maintaining academic integrity in a digital-first environment.


DepEd prohibits AI applications that pose significant risks to learner rights, such as biometric or emotion recognition systems. These manipulative technologies are deemed dangerous to the privacy and well-being of minors in the school system.

5. The Toll of Overreliance

In a masterclass titled "AI in the Classroom: Tool or Teacher," over 500 educators from 38 countries gathered to reflect on the psychological risks of automation.


Ann-Louise Davidson of Concordia University warned that excessive dependence on AI can lead to a loss of autonomy and creativity. When students rely solely on algorithms for output, they risk losing the very skills they are supposed to be developing.


Overreliance is also linked to increased levels of anxiety and isolation. AI-generated content can homogenize learning, erasing cultural diversity and the unique voices that students bring to the classroom through their personal experiences and heritage.


UNESCO stresses that AI should be reserved for repetitive tasks, allowing humans to focus on creative and complex projects. Teachers must continue to be recognized as mentors and guides, with AI serving only as a complement to their human role.


The session concluded that learning is, and should remain, a primarily human experience. Cultivating critical thinking is the only way to ensure that students control the technology, rather than being controlled by it.


Conclusion: Innovation must be balanced with humanity to prevent the digital age from becoming an era of intellectual stagnation.

6. Project AGAP.AI: Literacy for All

To combat the risks of the digital divide, DepEd initiated Project AGAP.AI to promote AI literacy and awareness among millions of Filipinos.


This initiative aims to train approximately 1.05 million learners, 300,000 teachers, and 150,000 parents. By partnering with the ASEAN Foundation and Google.org, DepEd is ensuring that the community understands the ethics of the tools they use.


Literacy training focuses on how to evaluate AI outputs responsibly. Participants learn to recognize biases in algorithms and understand how to protect their personal data from unauthorized collection by third-party AI providers.


The program also emphasizes the environmental impact of technology. Through projects like "Nature Positive AI," students are taught how digital transformation can align with sustainable development goals to protect the planet's future.


By involving parents in the training, the project ensures that the digital conversation continues at home. This holistic approach builds a community that is not only tech-savvy but also ethically grounded and socially responsible.


Conclusion: Education is the ultimate tool for navigating the AI revolution, turning potential threats into opportunities for growth.
